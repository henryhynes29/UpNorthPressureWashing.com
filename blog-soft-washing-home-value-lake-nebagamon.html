Why bay humidity is hard on Lake Nebagamon homes

Lake Nebagamon is wrapped by St. Louis, Lake Nebagamon, and Allouez Bays, plus two rivers. That water on nearly every side makes for some of the most humid, fog-prone air anywhere in the TWIn Ports. For your home's exterior, that means siding and shaded walls stay damp far longer than they would inland — and damp surfaces are exactly where algae and mold flourish.

You'll spot it across town, from the East End to Billings Park: green-black streaking that creeps up north-facing walls and shaded sections. It accumulates quietly until the whole home looks dingy.
